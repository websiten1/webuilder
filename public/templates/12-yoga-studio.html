— our studio

Small room. Long practice.

Replace this with how the studio came to be. The first class you taught in a borrowed room. The morning the lease came through.

"We opened the studio in Year on a simple bet: that there were enough people in this neighborhood who wanted to slow down."

Tell people what the room feels like. Wood floor, warm lamps, sandalwood somewhere in the corner. We teach four lineages but always come back to the breath.

Talk about your training, your teachers, and the long line of practice you stand inside of. Specifics are the only thing that prevents this from sounding like a brochure.

End with the part that's actually yours — what you hope someone takes home from a Tuesday morning class.
